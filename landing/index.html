The Problem

A gap that has never had
a financial instrument

Every logistics contract carries delivery SLA clauses. When shipments arrive late, companies absorb penalties, lose customers, and damage relationships — losses that standard cargo insurance simply does not cover. The risk is real, quantifiable, and entirely unhedged. Until now.

How It Works

Three signals. One market.

Backward-looking
Delivery Pulse™ History what has happened

60-minute rolling history, 14-day settlement record, probability distribution across risk bands. The evidence base — how volatile has delivery risk actually been.

Present
Delivery Pulse™ Index what is happening now

A live 0–100 signal updating every 10 seconds, derived from carrier performance, weather, traffic, and volume conditions. Drives all contract pricing in real time.

Forward-looking
Market-Implied Rate what the market expects

The price at which buyers and sellers agree to trade encodes a forward probability of late delivery. This market-consensus forecast is generated by trading activity — not a model.
